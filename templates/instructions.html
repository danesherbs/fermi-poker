Fermi Poker: Game Instructions

Objective: Win the most chips by making accurate estimates and strategic bets. Players take turns making Fermi estimates and betting on the accuracy of these estimates, similar to poker.

How to Play

  1. Taking Turns: In this two-player game, you'll alternate roles as the estimator and the bettor. When you're the estimator, you'll answer a Fermi question like "What's the average number of hairs on a person's head?"
  2. Making Your Estimate: Express your answer using scientific notation in the form of 10^(x ± y), where:
    • x is your central estimate (the exponent of 10)
    • y is your uncertainty range (in orders of magnitude)

    Example: If you estimate 1,000,000 hairs with an uncertainty of 2 orders of magnitude, enter 10^(6 ± 2). This means your estimate ranges from 10^4 (10,000) to 10^8 (100,000,000).

  3. Betting Round: After the estimate is made:
    • The non-estimating player starts by calling, raising, or folding
    • If they raise, the estimator can then call or fold
    • Betting continues until both players have called or someone folds
  4. Determining Accuracy: The true answer is revealed after betting concludes:
    • If the true answer falls within your estimated range, your estimate is accurate
    • If the true answer is outside your range, your estimate is inaccurate
  5. Payout Structure: The payout depends on both accuracy and precision:
    • Accurate estimate with narrow range (small y): Highest payout
    • Accurate estimate with wide range (large y): Lower payout
    • Inaccurate estimate with narrow range: Largest loss
    • Inaccurate estimate with wide range: Smaller loss

    If either player folds, the remaining player wins the current pot.

  6. Winning the Game: Play continues for an agreed-upon number of rounds. The player with the most chips at the end wins!

Strategic Tips

  • Balance precision with accuracy — narrower ranges (smaller y values) yield higher rewards but carry greater risk
  • Consider the difficulty of the question when deciding your uncertainty range
  • Watch your opponent's betting patterns to gauge their confidence
