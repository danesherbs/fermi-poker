Fermi Poker

Answer hard-to-Google questions, bet on your accuracy, and win chips. The more precise you are, the more you win — or lose.

The Core Loop

  1. The estimator sees a question (e.g. "How many hairs on a human head?") and submits an answer as a power of 10 with an uncertainty range: 10^(x ± y).
  2. The bettor sees the estimate and decides: raise, call, or fold.
  3. The true answer is revealed. If it falls inside the range, the estimator wins. If outside, the bettor wins.

Players alternate roles each round. The estimator antes 1 chip to start.

The Key Tradeoff

Your uncertainty range (± y) sets the payout multiplier. Narrower ranges pay more — but are harder to hit.

Range (± y) Multiplier Example
± 0 8x Exactly right power of 10
± 1 5x Within 1 order of magnitude
± 2 2x Within 2 orders of magnitude
± 3 1x Within 3 orders of magnitude

The winner gains ante × multiplier chips. The loser loses the same amount.

Betting

After the estimate is submitted, players alternate raising, calling, or folding — just like poker. If someone folds, their opponent wins the pot without revealing the answer.

Example

Question: "How many bones in the human body?" (true answer: ~200 = 10^2)

  • You estimate 10^(2 ± 1) — a range from 10 to 1,000.
  • The true answer (200) is inside your range, so you win.
  • With a ± 1 range, the 5x multiplier applies. If the ante was 2 chips, you win 10.
